How do organizations use IT to support transactions and business processes?

Organizations use IT (Information Technology) to support transactions and business processes in several ways. IT systems help streamline operations, improve accuracy, and enhance efficiency. Here are some common applications:

1. Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)

What It Does: TPS records day-to-day business transactions such as sales, payments, or inventory updates. These systems automate repetitive tasks and ensure accuracy.
Examples: Point-of-Sale (POS) Systems in retail automatically update inventory when a product is sold. Banking Systems process customer deposits, withdrawals, and fund transfers.
Benefits: Speed up transaction processing. Reduce errors by automating calculations and data entry.

2. Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) Systems

What It Does: ERP systems integrate core business processes like finance, HR, supply chain, and manufacturing into one platform.
Examples: Tools like SAP and Oracle ERP provide modules for tracking orders, managing resources, and maintaining financial records.
Benefits: Promotes real-time data sharing across departments. Enhances decision-making by providing a unified view of business operations.

Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

What It Does: CRM systems help manage customer interactions, sales data, and marketing campaigns.
Examples: Software like Salesforce and HubSpot track customer purchase history and preferences to personalize services.
Benefits: Builds stronger customer relationships. Increases sales efficiency and customer satisfaction.

4. E-Commerce Platforms

What It Does: IT powers online stores and payment systems, enabling businesses to sell products and services digitally.
Examples: Platforms like Shopify and WooCommerce integrate with payment processors and inventory systems for seamless transactions.
Benefits: Expands market reach beyond physical locations. Supports multiple payment methods for customer convenience.

5. Workflow Automation and Business Process Management (BPM)

What It Does: IT automates workflows to simplify and accelerate complex business processes.
Examples: Tools like Zapier or Monday.com automate repetitive tasks like sending invoices or updating customer records.
Benefits: Saves time and reduces manual errors. Allows employees to focus on higher-value tasks.

6. Supply Chain Management (SCM) Systems

What It Does: IT tracks goods as they move through the supply chain, from suppliers to customers.
Examples: Systems like Oracle SCM monitor inventory levels, shipping status, and supplier performance.
Benefits: Reduces costs by optimizing logistics. Prevents delays with real-time tracking.

7. Data Analytics and Reporting

What It Does: IT systems analyze transactional data to identify trends and improve decision-making.
Examples: Power BI and Tableau create visual dashboards to monitor business performance.
Benefits: Helps predict customer behavior. Optimizes operations based on insights.
